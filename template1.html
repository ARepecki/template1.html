Salmon and Cream Cheese Bagels (high protein)

Prep Details:

  • Prep Time: 7 mins
  • Cook Time: 3 mins
  • Total Time: 10 mins
  • Serving: 2

Pickled Cucumber:

  • 2 baby cucumbers
  • 2 tbsp cider vinegar
  • 1 tbsp evoo
  • pinch salt
  • pinch black pepper

    • Salmon and Cream Cheese Bagels:

    • 2 ptoeing bagel thins (sliced in half)
    • 2.5 oz cream cheese
    • 2 (3.5 oz) salmon filets

    Toppings:

    • 1 c (packed) arugula leaves
    • 2 tbsp hot sauce
    • 1 tbsp mixed black and white sesame seeds
    • salt to taste
    • pepper to taste

    Instructions:

    1. Chop cucumbers into bite-size pieces. Place in a small bowl.
    2. Pour the cider vinegar and oil over the cucumber. Sprinkle with salt and pepper, cider vinegar, evoo, salt, and pepper.
    3. Stir together and put to one side.
    4. Toast bagels to your liking. Place on two plates, spread cream cheese on bagels.
    5. Top with salmon and arugula.
    6. Add cucumber to the plates and pour any of the vingegar/oil mixture left in the bowl over the arugula.
    7. Drizzle hot sauce over the salmon and cucumber.
    8. Sprinkle on sesame seeds, salt, and pepper, then serve.

No-Knead Homemade English Muffins (No Oven Needed)

Prep:

  • Prep Time: 25 mins
  • Cook Time: 20 mins
  • Ferment For: 12 hrs
  • Total Time: 12 hrs 45 mins

Ingredients:

  • 2 1/2 c all-purpose flour
  • 1/4 tsp instant yeast
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 2/3 c whole milk
  • 1/2 c water
  • 1 tbsp butter, melted

Instructions:

  1. In a medium bowl, add the flour, yeast, and salt. Mix briefly.
  2. In a seperate (microwave safe) container, add milk and water. Pop into the microwave 15-30 seconds or until lukewarm. Stir in melted butter.
  3. Add the wet ingredients to the dry and mix until a dough forms. The dough will be soft and a little sticky.
  4. Once the dough has come together, just scrape down the sides of the bowl and cover tightly with cling wrap and a clean kitchen towel. Let sit at room temperature for 12-18 hours. (if you want to cook muffins at a later time pop the dough into the fridge after 18 hours).

The Next Day

  1. The dough will have doubled in size. Turn it out onto a floured surface. Cover with cling wrap and a kitchen towl and let rest for 10 minutes.
  2. After resting, the gluten will have relaxed. You can now roll it out to about 2 cm or slightly under 1-inch thick.
  3. Using a 3-inch cookie cutter dusted in flour, cut out rounds and transfer them to another baking tray lined with parchment. Any scraps can be re-rolled and made into another muffin. (At this point, you can place the muffins in the fridge to be cooked off later.)
  4. Loosely cover the muffins with plastic wrap and a tea towel and let them proof for about 40-45 minutes. After 45 minutes, the dough circles will have puffed up a little and will appear rounded. Proceed to cook.
  5. Heat a large nonstick frying pan over a steady, medium to medium-low heat.
  6. Using a flat spatula, carefully place the English muffins onto the heated frying pan about 2 inches apart and cover with a lid. (Makes 8 muffins at a time, so will need to cook off in 2 batches.)
  7. Cook on this side 6-7 minutes. The steam created with the lid on will help the muffins rise and cook through fully. Take extra care when moving muffins to avoid compressing the dough and knocking out bubbles.
  8. Flip over and cook for about another 3 minutes.
  9. Set aside the muffins to cool down on a wire rack before eating them fresh. These muffins toast up really well and make amazing breakfast sandwhiches.
  10. Store for 4 days at room temperature or freeze up to 8 weeks.
